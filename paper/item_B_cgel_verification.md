**comparative_more** — yaml "CGEL". CGEL ch.7 §2.2 [11]iii ("…analytic

**inflectional_comparison** — yaml "CGEL on inflected prepositions (near);

**right_mod** — yaml "CGEL preposition modification". CGEL ch.7 §2 [9]iii:
"A subset of prepositions are distinguished by their acceptance of such
adverbs as *right* and *straight* as modifiers." Also [11]v: "Central
prepositions accept *right* and *straight* as modifiers; adjectives do not."
Supports {prep: yes, adj: no}. ✅

**np_complement** — yaml "CGEL; Maling 1983". CGEL ch.7 §2.2 [11]iv: "Central
prepositions license NP complements; in general, adjectives do not." Plus
ch.7 §2.1 (l.18460): "only four adjectives [take NP complements], namely
*worth*, *due*, *like*, and *unlike*." ✅

**that_clause_complement** — yaml "CGEL". CGEL ch.7 §2 [9]i + (b) (l.~18465):
"Declarative content clauses are non-expandable if they do not permit the
subordinator *that*. Almost all words that license complements of this kind
are prepositions…". Note: CGEL frames the content-clause criterion as
preposition-distinguishing; the diagnostic's {adj: partial, prep: partial}
mapping should be reconciled with this. ✅ (with note for pm)

**become_complement** — yaml "CGEL". CGEL ch.7 §2.2 [11]ii: "AdjPs, other
than those restricted to attributive or postpositive function, can mostly
occur as complement to *become*; in general, PPs cannot." Supports
{adj: yes, prep: no}. ✅

**stranding** — yaml "CGEL". CGEL ch.7 §2.2 [11]vi: "Prepositions taking NP
complements can normally be fronted along with their complement in relative
and interrogative constructions … in general, adjectives cannot." (Stranding
proper is ch.7 §4.1, l.18423.) ✅

**fronting_pp** — yaml "CGEL information packaging chapter". Core statement is

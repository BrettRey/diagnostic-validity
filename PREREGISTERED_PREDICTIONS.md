# Pre-registered predictions (RQ3)

Registered **before instantiation** and before any rating exists; the git history
of this file is the timestamp. Per PLAN §5 outcome-neutrality these apply to the
pm node's *own* publications: if the grid contradicts a prediction, that is
reported as found, which is the strongest available signal that the deck is not
stacked.

Methodological basis: the models see an unlabelled lexeme × item matrix (F3).
Stratum labels are coverage devices, not ground truth, so an author's category
positions are *tested* here, not presupposed in how the sample is curated. That is
why evolved positions become predictions rather than sample edits.

## P1 -- more / less (registered 2026-06-11)

Reynolds (the *more*/*less*-are-determinatives paper) predicts that the diagnostic
profiles of *more* and *less* cluster with the determinative community, not with
adjectives or adverbs. Both lexemes are forced into the grid via the boundary set;
the test is which community their profiles join under the measurement models.

## Candidate parallel predictions (NOT yet registered -- await author wording)

The same falsifiable form applies to two further authorial positions. Their exact
wording must come from the source papers (source-grounding: not formulated from
memory), so they are listed as candidates, to be registered only once the author
supplies or confirms the precise claim:

- *what* / *which* are always determinatives (personhood-and-proforms paper):
  would predict their profiles cluster with determinatives rather than forming a
  separate pronoun community. Honesty caveat (ruling 2): whether residual
  pronoun-like behaviour is analysed as fused-head or as a distinct category is an
  analytical choice the data constrain but cannot decide alone.
- *us* / *we* / *you* as (personal) determinatives (determinatives-are-nouns
  paper): would predict their profiles sit with the determinative community.
